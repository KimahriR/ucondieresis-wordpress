# Content Guidelines

## Tone of Voice

Content should feel:
- Warm
- Elegant
- Human
- Premium
- Clear
- Close
- Honest

The tone should be natural in Mexican Spanish when writing final website copy.

## Brand Communication Style

The brand should communicate:
- Personalized attention
- Emotional value
- Careful craftsmanship
- Professional warmth
- Confidence without arrogance

The site should feel like a direct invitation to start a conversation, not like a mass-market sales machine.

## Messaging Direction

The brand communicates personalized attention, detail, and quality.

Focus on:
- Personalized products
- Emotional value
- Careful craftsmanship
- Direct communication through WhatsApp
- Inspiration and discovery
- Catalog exploration
- Gift moments and special occasions

Avoid:
- Generic corporate copy
- Overpromising
- Cold or technical language
- Typical e-commerce phrasing
- Pushy sales language
- Copy that sounds automated or templated

## Writing Principles

Prefer:
- Short clear headlines
- Warm and confident supporting copy
- Specific benefits over vague claims
- Natural phrasing in Mexican Spanish
- Copy that leads naturally toward contact

Avoid:
- Long blocks of ornamental text
- Empty luxury language
- Exaggerated claims
- Overly formal or distant tone

## CTA Guidelines

Preferred CTA style:
- `Solicitar catálogo`
- `Consultar disponibilidad`
- `Pedir información`
- `Enviar mensaje por WhatsApp`
- `Escríbenos por WhatsApp`
- `Crear regalo`

CTA tone should be:
- Direct
- Warm
- Clear
- Action-oriented without pressure

Avoid:
- `Buy now`
- `Add to cart`
- `Submit`
- `Checkout`
- Generic `Click here`

## Content Priorities by Section

### Homepage

The homepage should:
- Introduce the brand clearly
- Show product inspiration quickly
- Highlight featured products or categories
- Guide users toward catalogs and WhatsApp

### Product Content

Product-related copy should:
- Emphasize customization
- Suggest possibilities without overwhelming
- Support inquiry and quotation flow
- Avoid sounding like a fixed-price product listing

### Catalog Content

Catalog-related copy should:
- Encourage download
- Suggest breadth of options
- Reinforce that products are personalized

### Contact and WhatsApp

Contact copy should:
- Reduce friction
- Feel personal and welcoming
- Make it easy to start the conversation

## Headlines and Supporting Copy

Headlines should feel:
- Simple
- Elegant
- Emotional without being cheesy
- Premium without being stiff

Supporting copy should:
- Clarify what the user can do next
- Reinforce trust
- Keep the message easy to scan

## Language to Prefer

Prefer wording around:
- Personalized products
- Unique details
- Special moments
- Gifts with intention
- Ideas turned into meaningful products
- Personal attention
- Custom quotations
- Catalogs and inspiration

Avoid:
- Price-first messaging
- Store terminology
- Aggressive urgency
- Corporate slogans

## Language to Avoid

Avoid phrases or patterns that feel like:
- Generic online stores
- Marketplace listings
- Overwritten luxury branding
- Empty emotional promises

Examples to avoid:
- `Best prices`
- `Limited stock`
- `Fast checkout`
- `Shop now`
- `Premium solutions for your needs`

## Content Principles

Prefer:
- Short clear headlines
- Elegant and useful supporting copy
- Conversion-focused but honest messaging
- Natural section-to-section flow
- Copy that supports the visual experience

Avoid:
- Text that feels templated
- Excessively long blocks of marketing copy
- Language that makes the site feel like a mass-market store

## Consistency Notes

When creating new content:
- Keep terminology consistent across homepage, catalogs, and product pages
- Reuse CTA language intentionally
- Align visual tone and written tone
- Favor clarity over cleverness
